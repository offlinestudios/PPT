Important: New ICAO Photo Requirements for PCC — Effective September 1, 2025

The Government of India updated its photo requirements for Police Clearance Certificate applications effective September 1, 2025. Only ICAO-compliant photographs are now accepted. A further enforcement notice issued on December 1, 2025 made digital photo upload mandatory before any PCC application can be submitted online. Applications submitted without a compliant photo are rejected outright.

The PCC is explicitly listed alongside Passport, Surrender Certificate, and Global Entry Program as a document type requiring ICAO-compliant photos, as confirmed in official notices from Indian consulates worldwide.

Full PCC Photo Requirements

The following requirements apply to all Indian Police Clearance Certificate photo submissions as of September 2025. These are identical to the updated Indian passport photo requirements, as both documents now fall under the same ICAO standard:

  • Physical size: 35mm × 45mm (portrait format)
  • Digital dimensions: 630 × 810 pixels
  • Colour photograph only
  • Plain white background — no patterns, shadows, or off-white tones
  • Face must cover 80–85% of the photograph
  • Full head visible from top of hair to bottom of chin
  • Head centred within the frame — not tilted
  • Full face, front-facing view
  • Eyes open and clearly visible — no hair across the eyes
  • Neutral expression with mouth closed
  • Uniform lighting — no harsh shadows, flash reflections, or red-eye
  • Photo taken from approximately 1.5 metres from the camera
  • No glasses — must be removed to avoid reflections
  • No head coverings except for religious reasons (full face must be visible from chin to forehead)
  • Photo must be unaltered — no digital retouching, filters, or background edits
  • Recent photo taken within the last 6 months
  • Digital upload of photo and signature is mandatory before submitting the online application

Note on Digital Upload: As of December 1, 2025, the VFS Global and BLS Canada PCC application portals will not accept a submission unless the ICAO-compliant photo and applicant signature have been uploaded digitally beforehand. Our studio provides the 630 × 810 pixel digital file alongside your printed photos so you have everything needed for both the online upload and the physical submission.

How to Apply for an Indian PCC from Canada

Indian nationals residing in Canada can apply for a Police Clearance Certificate through BLS International Service Canada Inc., the official outsourcing partner of the Consulate General of India. The process involves both an online component and a physical submission.

  • 1
    Get your ICAO-compliant photos

    Visit Passport Photo Toronto to have your 35 × 45 mm printed photos and 630 × 810 px digital file prepared. Both are required for the application.

  • 2
    Complete the online application form

    Visit the BLS International Canada PCC portal and fill in the online application form. You will need your current Indian passport details, Canadian address, and the purpose of the PCC.

  • 3
    Upload your photo and signature digitally

    Before submitting the online form, you must upload your ICAO-compliant digital photo (630 × 810 px) and your digital signature. The application cannot be submitted without these uploads.

  • 4
    Print and sign the application form

    After online submission, download and print the completed application form. Sign it physically — the printed form still requires a handwritten signature.

  • 5
    Submit your documents

    Submit the signed printed form, your printed photos, a copy of your current Indian passport, and any supporting documents to a BLS centre in Toronto, Vancouver, Ottawa, or other Canadian cities, or by mail.

  • 6
    Receive your PCC

    Processing times vary based on the country for which the clearance is required. Standard processing typically takes 4–8 weeks. The PCC is issued by the Indian police authorities after verification of your Indian address history.

PCC vs. Indian Passport vs. OCI: Photo Differences

It is important to use the correct photo format for each document type, as the specifications differ. The table below summarises the key differences:

Requirement Indian Passport Police Clearance (PCC) OCI Card
Physical size 35 × 45 mm 35 × 45 mm 2 × 2 in (51 × 51 mm)
Digital dimensions 630 × 810 px 630 × 810 px 600 × 600 px
Aspect ratio Portrait (rectangular) Portrait (rectangular) Square
Background Plain white Plain white Plain white
Face coverage 80–85% 80–85% 50–70%
Glasses Not permitted Not permitted Not permitted
Digital upload Mandatory Mandatory Mandatory
